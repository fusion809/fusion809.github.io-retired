This is a how to guide on how to install the Android 9.0 (Pie) Pixel Experience ROM on the Nexus 5X phone. The original forum post that I largely based this on is here.


Initial steps


  1. Connect your phone to your PC via a USB-C cable.

  2. (If required) install Android tools from https://developer.android.com/studio/releases/platform-tools.html, or via the package manager of your Linux distribution. Specifically, you need the adb and fastboot tools.

  3. Backup your data either to the cloud, or by putting the phone into fastboot mode (power button + volume down button) and running: adb backup -all because the next step, unlocking the bootloader, will clear your device of all user data.

Unlock the bootloader

  1. Enable developer mode—in “Settings”, go to “About phone” and tap “Build number” ten times. The phone should say that you are now a developer. Within “Settings”, a menu called “Developer options” should appear now. Although, this may be tucked away within the “System” menu.

  2. Enable USB debugging and OEM unlock in “Developer options”.

  3. Enter fastboot mode, either by running: adb reboot bootloader in a terminal, or by pressing the volume down button and the power button at the same time.

  4. Once in fastboot mode, run the following to check whether your PC detects your phone: fastboot devices.

  5. Run fastboot flashing unlock in a terminal on your PC. This will trigger a data wipe, as a safeguard against the possibility that someone did this unlock in an effort to steal your private, otherwise encrypted data.

  6. The phone will probably reboot on its own, if it doesn’t, reboot it. Voilà, your phone should now be unlocked.

TWRP installation

  1. Download the latest TWRP from https://dl.twrp.me/bullhead.

  2. Reboot into fastboot mode, either by shutting your phone down and pressing volume down and power simultaneously, or by opening a terminal and running: adb reboot bootloader.

  3. Once in fastboot mode, run: fastboot devices in a terminal.

  4. Then, in a terminal, run: fastboot flash recovery twrp-<version string>.img fastboot boot twrp-<version string>.img.

  5. If your phone doesn’t reboot after this into recovery mode, turn off the phone and hold down both the volume down and power button simultaneously. Then select “Recovery Mode”.

ROM installation

  1. Download the required zip archive from https://download.pixelexperience.org/bullhead/ onto your PC. The file should have name of the format: PixelExperience_bullhead-9.0-<timestamp>-OFFICIAL.zip. At the time of writing, the <timestamp> of the latest zip archive is 20190823-1600.

  2. Tap the “Wipe” option on your phone.

  3. Select “Format Data”. This will delete all data, and remove encryption.

  4. (Not sure this option exists) tap “Advanced Wipe”, then select Cache and System partitions to be wiped and then swipe to wipe.

  5. On the phone, select “Advanced”, “ADB Sideload” and then swipe to begin sideload.

  6. On the PC, run: adb sideload PixelExperience_bullhead-9.0-<timestamp>-OFFICIAL.zip

  7. Run: adb reboot to restart your phone. You should notice an aesthetic change to practically every aspect of your phone’s software, including its splash screen, home screen, settings app, app screen, etc.
